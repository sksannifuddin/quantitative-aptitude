## Key Concepts Learned

- **Natural Numbers (N):** {1, 2, 3, ...} — counting numbers, closed under addition/multiplication, not under subtraction/division
- **Whole Numbers (W):** Natural numbers + 0
- **Integers (Z):** Positive, negative numbers, and zero — includes sign rules for multiplication:

  | Sign | Result |
  |------|--------|
  | + × + | + |
  | − × − | + |
  | + × − | − |
  | − × + | − |

- **Rational Numbers (Q):** Expressible as p/q (q ≠ 0) — includes terminating and repeating decimals (e.g., 0.333... = 1/3)
- **Irrational Numbers:** Non-terminating, non-repeating decimals (√2, π, e) — square roots of non-perfect squares are always irrational
- **Real Numbers (R):** Rational ∪ Irrational
- **Complex Numbers:** a + bi form, where i = √−1; powers of i cycle every 4:
  - i¹ = i
  - i² = −1
  - i³ = −i
  - i⁴ = 1

## Important Patterns for Aptitude

- Even + Even = Even
- Odd + Odd = Even
- Odd + Even = Odd

**Number system hierarchy:**
```
Complex Numbers
└── Real Numbers
    ├── Rational Numbers
    │   ├── Integers
    │   │   └── Whole Numbers
    │   │       └── Natural Numbers
    │   └── Fractions
    └── Irrational Numbers
```
